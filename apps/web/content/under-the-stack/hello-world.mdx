## What AlphaScan Actually Does

You enter a URL. Ninety seconds later, you know everything that's happening — and going wrong — under the hood of your marketing.

We're not checking if you have a meta description and calling it a day. We run **45 specialized analysis modules** across 8 scoring categories that examine:

- Whether your analytics are actually tracking (or silently dropping data)
- Whether your ad pixels are firing on the right pages (or wasting your budget)
- Whether your cookie banner is legally compliant (or a €2.8M liability)
- Whether your marketing tools are working together (or 4 of them are abandoned)
- Whether your security headers protect your brand (or let anyone spoof your emails)
- How your traffic, rankings, and competitive position compare to your industry
- Whether your website is accessible to the 15% of users with disabilities
- How much dead software is loading on every single page view

The result is a **MarketingIQ score** — a single number from 0 to 100 that tells you how well your marketing technology stack is actually performing. Not how pretty your website is. How well the invisible infrastructure behind it is working.

This is the audit you'd pay an agency $5,000–$150,000 for. We deliver it before your coffee gets cold.

## Why Now

The marketing technology landscape has never been more chaotic. The average website loads 14+ third-party scripts. Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, DMA) have turned consent compliance into a minefield where the fines are real and the rules keep changing. And the explosion of AI means we can finally synthesize raw data into strategic intelligence — not just checklists, but actual analysis of what's broken and what to do about it.

The cost of NOT knowing is going up every quarter. GDPR fines average €2.8M. CCPA violations cost $2,500–$7,500 each. A broken Meta pixel can silently waste thousands in ad spend per month. An abandoned MarTech tool adds latency to every page view while you keep paying the subscription.

The information asymmetry between "people who know what's happening on a website" and "people paying for marketing" has been the most expensive gap in digital business for a decade.

We're closing it.
